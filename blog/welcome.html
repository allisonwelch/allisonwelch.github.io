Welcome to My Corner of the Internet

Welcome! I'm thrilled to launch this new website and blog as a space to share my thoughts, research, and curiosities. After years of working quietly in the lab and in meetings, I decided it was time to put my voice out there more publicly. This blog is where I'll do that.

You'll find a lot of content here about Arctic biogeochemistry and climate change—the core of my research as a scientist. I study how warming Arctic ecosystems are changing and what that means for global biogeochemical cycles. It's fascinating, urgent, and deeply important work. But beyond the nitty-gritty details of my papers, I want to write about the bigger picture: why this science matters, what we can do about it, and how climate change intersects with policy, economics, and society.

Beyond the science, you'll also find my reflections on science policy, the changing landscape of academic research, and the cultural and political issues that capture my attention. I believe that scientists have a responsibility to engage with the broader public conversation. We have insights to offer, but we also need to listen and learn from different perspectives. This is my attempt to do both.

I hope you'll stick around. Whether you're here because you're interested in Arctic science, climate policy, or just want to get to know me a bit better, I'm grateful you stopped by. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to chat about any of these topics—I'd love to hear from you.

Happy reading,
Allison
